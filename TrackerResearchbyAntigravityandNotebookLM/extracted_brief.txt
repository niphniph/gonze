3. Industry and Market Requirements
Antigravity should treat this as a product strategy + implementation assignment. The platform sits at the intersection of five markets:
Productivity and planner apps: Notion, Todoist, TickTick, Sunsama, Motion, Akiflow, ClickUp, Asana.
AI scheduling and calendar intelligence: Reclaim, Motion, Clockwise, Calendly, Google Calendar workflows.
Meeting automation: Google Meet, Zoom, Fireflies, Otter, Fathom, meeting assistants.
Email productivity: Gmail, Superhuman, Spark, Front, workflow-based follow-up tools.
Personal finance and budgeting: Rocket Money, YNAB, Monarch Money, Copilot Money, Plaid-powered apps, open banking tools.
Market direction to design for: 
Productivity tools are increasingly judged by automation depth, integration quality, AI assistance, and whether they reduce manual planning work. For finance, users expect secure account linking, automatic categorization, budget recommendations, and plain-English insights rather than raw transaction lists.

7. Feature Module A: Google Calendar, Google Meet, and Gmail
7.1 Required Capabilities
Google OAuth connection flow with clear consent and permissions explanation.
Integration settings page showing connection status, granted scopes, sync status, and disconnect button.
Calendar event creation from daily plan, weekly plan, task, reminder, or meeting object.
Automatic Google Meet link creation for meeting-type events.
Participant invitation flow with attendee email addresses.
Gmail invitation or follow-up email workflow when a plan item requires communication.
Error handling for missing permissions, expired tokens, invalid attendee emails, timezone problems, and API failures.
7.2 UX Requirements
In every task/plan creation form, include toggles: Add to Google Calendar, Add Google Meet link, Send email invitation.
If the user has not connected Google, show a non-blocking Connect Google Account call-to-action.
If the task includes a participant email, suggest sending an invitation.
After event creation, show the calendar event status and Meet link.
Support editing or deleting linked events where technically possible.

9. Security, Privacy, and Compliance Requirements
Never store Google, Gmail, or banking passwords.
Use OAuth or provider-approved token flows only.
Encrypt access tokens, refresh tokens, and sensitive financial identifiers at rest.
Use HTTPS in production and secure cookies/session handling.
Use least-privilege scopes: request only permissions needed for the feature.
Add clear consent screens explaining what data is accessed and why.
Add disconnect/revoke access flows for Google and finance providers.
Add delete imported financial data option.
Log integration errors without exposing tokens or sensitive data.
Create a privacy notice page or modal for calendar, email, and financial data.
